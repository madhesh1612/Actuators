Different Ways Actuators Get Power

Actuators are grouped by how they get their energy. Hydraulic systems are very strong, while electric actuators are very exact. Air-powered (pneumatic) systems are fast and simple.

Electric Actuators: Flexible and Precise

Electric actuators are the most common and improving type. They are best for jobs needing exact control and clean conditions. Many types exist, each made for specific tasks.

Spotlight: How Magnetic Actuators Work

Magnetism's Power: The Lorentz Force in Action

Magnetic actuators work using a basic rule: the Lorentz Force. An electric current creates a magnetic field, which then pushes or pulls another magnet to make things move without touching.

Solenoid vs. Voice Coil: Which is Best?

Solenoids are built for simple, strong actions, while Voice Coil Actuators (VCAs) are great for fast, very exact control.
